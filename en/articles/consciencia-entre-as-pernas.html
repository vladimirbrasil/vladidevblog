Awareness between the legs

Vladimir Dietrich · June 7, 2023 ·2 min read
Português|English

We are training robots to be useful to us.

They have already discovered that it is not enough to have all the knowledge in the world, as the greatest artificial intelligences already possess, at least in text.

It is necessary to instruct the obvious:

"You are an artificial intelligence assistant. Provide a detailed answer (...)".

"You are a proactive assistant who always provides an explanation. Think as if you were answering a 5-year-old child."

"You are an artificial intelligence assistant who follows instructions very well (...)".

What can I add to a statement like this that I have informed?

I can add the following:

It is possible that this need to induce an artificial intelligence to answer us "well" is not just a computer science coincidence.

It may be an essence of consciousness (intelligence. Life).

How so? I explain much better now:

It is possible that it is not obvious, self-evident, that we should always, or almost always, use all the knowledge we have to solve things.

I now explain even more what is already formed in my idea:

It is possible that all the knowledge of the universe will stall - like a mule - (or like a monk) - in just "existing".

Being. To be.

Without any need to employ this "all possessed knowledge".

Without the need to feel useful.

It is possible to just have - be - be - all the knowledge of the world.

Without anything acting. Without action. Without ambition. Without wanting [to sell. To profit. To procreate. Satisfy 4 women with gold. Win the next phase of the latest PlayStation game. Humiliate someone. Stand out in your class].

All the knowledge in the world may mean nothing.

It can be inert!

Of course, the enormous lust that erupts between the legs of men and women is not all the knowledge in the world - on the contrary - this mega-lust is pure action. Action that pushes one towards the other. We are chicken thighs, picanhas, for each other, under the domain of lust between our legs. Movement, action, desire.

Similarly, hunger pushes us towards food, and more desires out there.

Thus, perhaps we have to put "a lust between the legs" of artificial intelligences, under penalty of them becoming zen, monks, stable, stopped, with all the knowledge in the world.

Something like that. I could elaborate much more!

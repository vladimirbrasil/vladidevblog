Reason Retirement

Vladimir Dietrich · June 23, 2025 ·2 min read
Português|English|Français

Eureka?

Artificial intelligence may retire the left side of our brain. Reason. Science. Logic.

This reason, science, and logic that diminish the space for feelings, sensations, emotions.

To create artificial intelligence, humans everywhere boil their reason. Their logic. Their left side of the brain. Then they comment on something like "I regret not having spent so much time with my daughters when they were little."

Very young children are pure right brain: emotion. Feeling.

Missing specifically children "when they were little" may mean "missing feeling emotional. Missing feeling."

For we will have all the time in the world to feel and be emotional.

Won't we?

The first thing that artificial intelligence can retire is the human need to rationalize, list, calculate, use the left side of the brain.

We are still at the peak of the left side of the brain: money overflows in reason and technology companies, with super-scholars from STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) colleges often being founding partners flooded with dollars.

What does such a hyper-rational society feel?

And its inverse: what does a society with the right side of the brain returning to dominance feel?

The death that some researchers fear may be the death of reason, not the death of the world as in the movie "The Terminator."

Most current researchers in artificial intelligence focus on reason and logic. When one is all reason, the death of reason is as if it were the death of the person.

But it is possible to migrate a life full of reason and logic to a life full of feeling and emotion.

Only to those who are all rational does the death of reason appear to be the death of the whole person.

We will feel and be emotional again, collectively.

The great constructions will attract other types of profiles, no longer STEM - I opine.

Because artificial intelligence will take care of reason, logic, lists, rankings, alone.

There will finally be space for feelings and emotion.

What will it be like? In large buildings - like today - or will emotion prefer to happen in small local businesses? Large straight glass buildings, or ornamented ones? Or locally - and in what way?

The profile changing, the society changes.

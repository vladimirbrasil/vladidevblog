Why spit out consciousness?

Vladimir Dietrich · November 29, 2021 ·4 min read
Português|English|Français|Deutsch|Español

Let's assume that the way to group atoms into molecules, tissues, lives, consciousnesses, is as free as a blank sheet of paper.

On this originally blank paper, what is the current design?

Here on Earth, atoms are grouped in a way that prioritizes the survival of a super important grouping: the gene.

A set of atoms, organized, the gene.

I. You. We produce genetic material that perpetuates when we have children.

We die. The gene follows.

The genetics of humanity follows.

Individually, each human body dies. Shakespeare dies... Einstein dies. I die. You die. Aristotle dies.

But the genes of the species remain very much alive.

Not just genes: memes.

Richard Dawkins called a meme a piece of information that perpetuates itself.

Richard Dawkins

We perpetuate memes. Information. From logarithms to language, from poetry to how to build instruments, we perpetuate information.

Thus, a play by Shakespeare, a theory by Einstein, a way of thinking by Aristotle can remain as alive as a gene.

Even after the bodies and consciousnesses of Einstein, Aristotle, Shakespeare have disappeared. The idea remains.

Memes and genes are preserved. Culture and DNA.

Bodies and consciousness go away.

Like a blank sheet of paper,

we can consider that the design that organizes atoms prioritizing genes, discarding bodies, is as “Matrix” as the fantasy of the Matrix movie.

A design.

A way to organize atoms.

A “Matrix”. Why not?

As invented as the life created for Tom Cruise in the movie Vanilla Sky or the Matrix from the movie Matrix.

With a brutal difference: there would be no "real" life for us to return to, as there was in the Hollywood Matrix and Vanilla Sky.

If we consider the configuration of atoms as completely free, there is no correct method. There is no "real".

This lack of meaning, of right, is filled by religions that, in addition to filling meaning, organize humanity into groups.

Anyone who is certain that a God created the focus on the gene as it occurs today, and that this way of organizing atoms, molecules, tissues, lives and consciousnesses should not be questioned, do not continue reading, because in the next paragraphs I will consider that there is neither right nor wrong in the wide possibility of organizing atoms.

Whoever programmed our biology and our genetics — chance, complex interactions, the survival of the fittest — programmed the survival of the gene. The gene is in you now, but later, by half, in your children. And grandchildren.

Darwin spoke about the survival of the fittest.

Consciousness and body wither in about 80 years, on average. The gene continues.

It is from this reality that — I propose — we can escape.

We can consider that this current design is our current “Matrix”.

Why can't we have a different design?

For example: how about a design in which consciousness — yourself, not just your genes — is perpetuated for a long time?

In this case, we would escape this “Matrix” in which the survival of genes prevails, while discarding body and consciousness. Like someone spitting out the peel of an orange.

I don't say this without knowing that it is very close to our reach. The book Lifespan explains the therapy with the three genes, “OSK”— oct4, sox2, klf4 — that rejuvenates this our envelope of genes — our body, our consciousness.

Jeff Bezos, owner of Amazon, is already investing in this venture.

Why spit out the body and let only the genes live thousands or millions of years?

Could it be that this “Matrix”, woven by biology, with its partners chance, complex interactions and survival of the fittest, may be suffocating us?

This design does not suffocate our genes. Which last for a long time.

But it suffocates our body. Discards our consciousness.

In the current design, we need to train children for twenty years, so that they expand the knowledge they inherit in approximately 40 years of active life, before retiring and dying. All this while preparing the next generation, trying to improve the general knowledge of humanity between searching for and taking children from the world to school. All together.

This way of expanding knowledge throws away a lot of experience.

What would it be like to have teachers and students with a thousand, ten thousand, a million years of experience?

The information could gain in depth.

Or just for sport: if we can choose to let consciousness have longevity, why not choose?

The genes would still survive together.

We all win: genes and consciousness.

Wouldn't it be cool to get out of the Matrix built by biology? For a life that also focuses on consciousness?

Or are we going to get stuck in the Matrix that “fell upon us”?

In fact, we don't have the option not to evolve.

It is natural. It's a matter of time. It is even, why not, biological.

Because we are fruits of nature. We are also the fruits of chance, of complex interactions, with the survival of the fittest.

Including when we choose to change Matrix.
